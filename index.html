DEEP LEARNING WITH MATLAB

Deep Learning with MATLAB is a course that teaches the fundamentals of deep learning using MATLAB software. The course covers topics such as neural networks, convolutional neural networks, recurrent neural networks, and deep reinforcement learning. Participants will learn how to create and train deep learning models, perform data preprocessing, and evaluate model performance using MATLAB. The course also covers advanced topics such as transfer learning, hyperparameter tuning, and feature extraction. By the end of the course, participants will have gained a strong understanding of deep learning fundamentals and be able to apply these skills to real-world problems

Location: College Auditorium
9:30 - 4:30PM

DEEP LEARNING WITH NLP

Deep Learning with NLP is a subfield of AI that focuses on enabling machines to understand and generate human language through the use of advanced algorithms and neural networks. It involves analyzing and interpreting vast amounts of unstructured data in the form of text, speech, and images to improve accuracy over time. Applications include machine translation, sentiment analysis, chatbots, and voice assistants. Challenges include handling complex syntax and semantics, cultural and linguistic differences, and the need for massive amounts of data. Despite these challenges, Deep Learning with NLP is a rapidly growing and exciting field with endless possibilities for innovation and development.

Location: College Auditorium
9:30 - 4:30PM

Object Segmentation With Deep Learning

Object segmentation with deep learning is a computer vision technique that aims to identify and separate objects from their surrounding background. It uses deep neural networks to analyze and process images pixel by pixel, identifying the boundaries of objects within an image. This technology has numerous practical applications, including medical imaging, self-driving cars, and object recognition in security systems. Deep learning allows for more accurate segmentation results by automatically learning and adapting to different object shapes and sizes, reducing the need for manual intervention. Despite its complexity, object segmentation with deep learning is becoming increasingly accessible and widespread, making it an exciting and promising area of research and development.

Location: College Auditorium

About Our College

The Government College, Kasaragod was established in1957 by the Government of Kerala, as a need was felt for a college in this underdeveloped area. In the beginning there was no facility of its own, so the classes were conducted in a local high school in Kasaragod town. It was the then Education Minister Prof. Joseph Mundasseri who took the initiative in the matter. The College commenced functioning early in August 1957 in a corner of the High School building. Prof. V. Gopalan Nair took over as the first Principal of the college on 08-07-1957. The College was declared open by the Honorable Minister of Finance, Sri. C. Achutha Menon on 07-08-1957. As of now the college offers 14 undergraduate courses, 7 post graduate courses and 5 PhD programmes. College is reaccredited with NAAC ‘A’ grade.
